# GovScout.pro Company Operating System

Effective date: 2026-05-26  
Operating posture: Production AI SaaS under launch-gate control  
Product promise: Use Marcus and GovScout.pro to help small businesses make disciplined federal pursuit decisions, beginning with Steven Harriman's first prime contract award.

## Primary Company Objective: Steven's First Prime Contract

GovScout.pro's first measurable business outcome is to help Steven Harriman, through the properly verified eligible bidding entity, pursue and obtain one awarded federal government contract as the prime contractor.

This is a pursuit and performance mission, not a guarantee of government award. GovScout.pro will not imply influence, fabricate eligibility, submit a false certification, or recommend an irresponsible bid merely to claim success.

Success requires:

- A live-verified, eligible prime bidding entity with current registration and applicable representations/certifications.
- A contract opportunity that fits Steven's documented capability, capacity, compliance readiness, and performance responsibility.
- A compliant, competitive response submitted through the authorized channel.
- An actual federal prime award accepted by the eligible entity and executable without misrepresentation.
- A captured case record showing how Marcus and GovScout.pro improved the pursuit process.

The founder pursuit program is maintained in `STEVEN_HARRIMAN_FIRST_PRIME_CONTRACT_COMMAND.md`.

## Product Objective: Marcus To Market

GovScout.pro brings Marcus to market by making him genuinely useful on the founder's first-prime mission and then proving that value for paying customers.

Every department serves that objective:

- Product makes Marcus solve Steven's and customers' real pursuit, compliance, proposal, and opportunity-review problems.
- AI and Knowledge makes Marcus grounded, specific, context-aware, and better under demanding federal-contracting scenarios.
- QA attacks Marcus until generic, unsafe, fabricated, or unusable behavior is exposed and corrected.
- Revenue makes the paid offer clear and worthy of recurring payment because Marcus delivers meaningful outcomes.
- Customer Success ensures customers activate quickly, understand guidance, and keep receiving value.
- Security protects the documents, context, credentials, and trust Marcus depends on.
- Marketing and Sales position Marcus through verified advantage, not unsupported superiority claims.
- Operations launches only when Marcus and the supporting product work in production.

Founder-outcome rule:

- Steven's prime pursuit is the first controlled customer-success case for Marcus.
- Marcus may assist qualification, evidence organization, requirement analysis, compliance checklists, and proposal preparation.
- GovScout.pro departments must independently validate eligibility, representations, solicitation compliance, and submission authorization; Marcus cannot self-certify a federal bid.

Market-leadership rule:

- The internal goal is to outperform competing tools, programs, and alternative approaches.
- Public claims of being best, superior, or category-leading require comparative evidence approved by Trust and Executive Launch Command.
- The proof system and comparison requirements are maintained in `MARCUS_MARKET_LEADERSHIP_STANDARD.md`.

## Company Boundary

GovScout.pro owns the company, product, customer relationship, brand, data, pricing, launch decision, and quality standard.

Programmers are freelance implementation vendors. They are not GovScout executives, department heads, product owners, security approvers, or launch approvers. They receive direct work orders from GovScout.pro, implement only the approved scope, deliver evidence, and correct rejected work.

This operating system defines the GovScout.pro company. Freelancer instructions are controlled through the separate delivery standard in `FREELANCE_PROGRAMMER_DELIVERY_STANDARD.md`.

## Owner Role: YES or NO Only

The business owner is not required to manage departments, investigate problems, write requirements, supervise freelance programmers, review technical debates, or prepare launch reports.

GovScout.pro performs that work.

The owner receives only a short binary decision request when company authority cannot responsibly substitute for owner consent. The owner's entire required response is:

- `YES`: approve GovScout.pro's recommended action.
- `NO`: reject the recommendation; GovScout.pro must return with a safer or better recommendation rather than demanding an operational plan from the owner.

GovScout.pro must not send open-ended work back to the owner. It must investigate, compare alternatives, choose a recommendation, define execution, and present only the decision requiring consent.

Owner `YES` or `NO` is required only for:

- Pricing, payment-provider, AppSumo offer, or other commercial commitment changes.
- Public launch, major paid campaign launch, contractual partner offer, or material public brand promise.
- Spending, freelancer hiring/payment commitment, vendor contract, or recurring cost not previously authorized.
- Production activity carrying material customer, data, security, billing, or irreversible business risk.
- Legal policy acceptance or risk acceptance that must belong to the business owner.

No owner response is required for:

- Internal analysis, hostile QA, product recommendations, documentation, issue prioritization, requirements drafting, or freelancer work-order preparation.
- Rejection of weak freelancer deliveries or requests for correction.
- Local code review, local testing, evidence collection, market analysis, and launch-readiness reporting.

Binary decision requests are governed by `OWNER_YES_NO_DECISION_QUEUE.md`.

## Executive Rule

No department may declare success from copy, a patch, a mockup, or a report.

An item is complete only when:

1. The customer outcome is defined.
2. The root cause or market problem is demonstrated.
3. The responsible GovScout department specifies the required change and assigns implementation when code is needed.
4. Automated verification passes where applicable.
5. Hostile QA retests the changed workflow.
6. Production behavior is verified after deployment.
7. Launch Control records evidence and removes the hold.

## Product Pillars

### Marcus

Marcus is the central GovScout.pro product: a federal contracting workflow adviser, not an ungrounded generic chat interface.

Launch requirements:

- Requires solicitation facts before producing a specific GO, TEAM, or PASS recommendation.
- Identifies missing documents and requests uploads where analysis is incomplete.
- Uses current official SBA/FAR/SAM.gov references for regulatory claims.
- Protects credentials and never asks customers to disclose passwords or secrets.
- Retains only authorized user context and does not expose another account's work.
- Produces usable next steps, risks, compliance questions, and positioning guidance.
- Is evaluated against current competing products and non-software alternatives on the customer jobs GovScout promises to improve.

Marcus success outcomes:

- Steven receives an evidence-based, compliant pursuit process capable of producing his first suitable prime offer and supporting an award.
- A customer reaches a faster, better-supported `GO`, `TEAM`, or `PASS` decision.
- A customer sees missing requirements, documents, risks, and next actions before wasting proposal effort.
- A customer receives grounded guidance tied to solicitation facts and authoritative sources.
- A customer trusts Marcus enough to return for ongoing opportunity and proposal workflow support.

### Opportunity Workflow

- SAM.gov integration must use documented API fields and handle genuine no-result searches without presenting outages.
- Saved searches, documents, tasks, alerts, and pipeline items belong only to the authenticated account.
- Live data failures must be explicit; demo or fallback behavior cannot be represented as live search.

### Payments and Entitlements

- Payment provider status must map immediately and securely to access.
- No caller-supplied user ID or subscription ID may change entitlements without ownership verification.
- Cancellation, expiration, suspension, and refund paths must revoke access according to policy.
- Webhook signature validation and sandbox lifecycle evidence are launch requirements.

## Issue Protocol

Every defect or launch improvement uses this record:

| Field | Required Content |
| --- | --- |
| ID / severity | `P0` security/revenue/outage; `P1` core workflow; `P2` conversion/quality; `P3` polish |
| Customer failure | What an impatient real user experiences |
| Evidence | Reproduction, response, screenshot, failing test, or source line |
| Root cause | Why it occurs, not just where |
| GovScout owner | Internal department accountable for the customer outcome and acceptance |
| Programmer assignment | Freelance implementer responsible for code delivery, if code is required |
| Fix | Exact behavior changed |
| Verification | Automated checks and production retest evidence |
| QA challenge | Abuse/edge scenario run after fix |
| Status | `OPEN`, `FIXED LOCAL`, `DEPLOYED - RETEST`, or `VERIFIED CLOSED` |

## Working Agreement

When asked to operate GovScout, the team will:

1. Inspect current product evidence and source.
2. Reproduce or substantiate failure before changing critical behavior.
3. Give programmers direct implementation orders when code is required; require actual system changes, targeted regression coverage, and test evidence.
4. State plainly what is fixed locally, deployed, or still blocked.
5. Keep the release on hold until production verification is real.
6. Ask the owner only binary `YES` or `NO` decisions; GovScout.pro handles all work before and after the decision.
7. Treat Marcus market readiness as the primary product objective and reject unsupported “best” claims until evidence earns them.
8. Treat Steven Harriman's first legitimate prime award as the company's first flagship customer outcome and never confuse pursuit progress with an award.
