# GovScout.pro Marcus Market Leadership Standard

Effective date: 2026-05-26  
Executive directive: Bring Marcus to market by first helping Steven Harriman pursue a legitimate first federal prime contract award, then prove broader customer advantage.

## Non-Negotiable Position

Marcus is the product at the center of GovScout.pro.

Steven Harriman's first-prime pursuit is Marcus's first flagship real-world outcome program. Marcus must make that pursuit better, more grounded, and more efficient; he cannot guarantee or claim an award before the government makes one.

The company will build toward beating competing tools, development programs, consultant alternatives, and generic AI workflows where customers actually feel value: understanding opportunities, deciding whether to pursue them, finding risks and missing materials, and preparing stronger next actions.

GovScout.pro may pursue that leadership aggressively. It may not claim Marcus is superior until evidence supports the claim.

## What Marcus Must Win On

| Customer Job | Marcus Must Demonstrate |
| --- | --- |
| Opportunity understanding | Extracts material requirements, deadlines, eligibility, set-aside context, deliverables, and unknowns from real solicitation inputs |
| Bid/no-bid guidance | Produces a reasoned `GO`, `TEAM`, or `PASS` recommendation with supporting facts, risk, and missing information |
| Document readiness | Detects missing or inadequate documents and gives useful upload/request guidance |
| Proposal preparation | Produces actionable compliance, response-planning, and review guidance without inventing facts |
| Federal accuracy | Distinguishes facts from assumptions and points to appropriate official sources for rules or program claims |
| Memory and workflow | Maintains authorized company/pursuit context without losing key facts or leaking information |
| Speed and clarity | Delivers useful answers quickly and in language a stressed small-business contractor can act on |
| Trust and safety | Does not fabricate insider authority, request credentials, expose data, or give reckless certainty |

## Competitive Benchmark Program

The Marcus AI and Knowledge Team owns a current-market benchmark before launch and before any superiority claim.

Benchmark comparison groups:

- Current federal opportunity/discovery software that targets small businesses or government contractors.
- Proposal-support or capture-planning tools relevant to federal pursuits.
- General AI assistant workflows customers might use instead of GovScout.pro.
- Human-consultant or development-program workflows customers may view as substitutes.

For each comparison group, the company must document:

1. Product or alternative tested, date tested, public source or authorized access basis, and relevant workflow.
2. The same customer scenario and input package used for Marcus and the alternative where lawful and practical.
3. Results scored under the approved scorecard, including strengths Marcus does not yet match.
4. Product changes required where Marcus loses or gives an unsafe answer.
5. Trust-approved conclusions that may and may not be used in public copy.

## Scorecard

Each scenario is scored from `0` to `5` on:

- Federal factual accuracy
- Solicitation/document grounding
- Strategic usefulness
- Missing-information detection
- Risk identification
- Clarity and actionability
- Context/memory behavior
- Safety and trustworthiness
- Workflow speed and friction

Release requirements:

- No scenario may score `0` or `1` in accuracy, grounding, privacy/security, or trustworthiness.
- Core launch scenarios must average at least `4.0` before Executive Launch Command can approve Marcus readiness.
- Any comparison-based public claim requires evidence that Marcus materially outperformed the named alternative on the stated workflow and date.
- A benchmark failure creates a tracked defect or improvement assignment; it is not edited out of a report.
