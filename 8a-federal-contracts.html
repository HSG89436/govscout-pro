// THE 8(a) ADVANTAGE

Why 8(a) Is the Program
Everyone Wants to Get Into

The SBA 8(a) Business Development program gives certified small disadvantaged businesses a nine-year window of protected federal contracting access — including something no other set-aside program offers: sole-source awards.

⚡ The sole-source advantage

8(a) firms can receive federal contracts without competition up to $4.5 million for services and $7.5 million for manufacturing. A contracting officer can award directly to your firm — no bid required. This is the most powerful contracting tool available to any small business certification.

What the 8(a) program provides

  • Set-aside contracts restricted to 8(a) participants
  • Sole-source awards below the threshold (no competition)
  • 9-year program participation window (4-year developmental stage + 5-year transition stage)
  • Access to SBA mentorship and business development resources
  • Ability to form joint ventures with other 8(a) firms under the SBA's All Small Mentor-Protégé Program

Who qualifies for 8(a)?

  • Socially and economically disadvantaged individuals (SBA-defined categories, including many minority groups)
  • At least 51% owned and controlled by the qualifying individual(s)
  • Personal net worth under $850,000 (excluding primary residence and business equity)
  • Meet SBA size standards for your primary NAICS code
  • Demonstrated potential for success
  • Not previously participated in the 8(a) program

// FAQ

Common Questions

How long does 8(a) certification take?
▾
SBA targets 90 days for 8(a) application processing, but actual timelines vary based on application completeness and SBA workload. Applications are submitted through certify.sba.gov. Common delays involve incomplete documentation — financial statements, tax returns, personal history. A complete, well-documented application processes faster.
Can I be both 8(a) and SDVOSB?
▾
Yes. Multiple certifications are common and beneficial. An SDVOSB who is also 8(a) certified can compete on SDVOSB set-asides, VOSB set-asides, and 8(a) set-asides — significantly expanding your eligible opportunity pool. GovScout Pro lets you set all your certifications and scores each opportunity accordingly.
What happens when my 9 years are up?
▾
When you graduate from the 8(a) program, you lose access to 8(a) set-asides and sole-source opportunities. The idea is that 9 years provides enough growth to compete in open markets. Many firms use the 8(a) period to build past performance, win IDIQ vehicles, and establish agency relationships that sustain the business after graduation.
Does GovScout Pro help with the 8(a) application?
▾
Marcus can explain the 8(a) eligibility requirements, walk you through what documentation is needed, and help you understand the application process. He cannot make legal or compliance determinations about your specific eligibility — for that, consult an SBA resource partner or government contracting attorney.
