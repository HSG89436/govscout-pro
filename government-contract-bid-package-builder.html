// HOW TO BUILD YOUR BID PACKAGE

A Repeatable Process
for Every Opportunity

Complete your business profile

Enter your company details in Marcus's business profile: NAICS codes, certifications, past performance history, key personnel, core capabilities, and insurance information. This takes 15–20 minutes once and Marcus uses it for every bid package you build.

Upload the solicitation package

Download the full solicitation from SAM.gov and upload it to GovScout Pro. Include the RFP/RFQ, Statement of Work, Performance Work Statement, and any Section L (Instructions) and Section M (Evaluation Criteria) documents. The more you upload, the more specific and compliant Marcus's outputs will be.

Ask Marcus to build your compliance matrix

Start with: "Build a compliance matrix for this solicitation." Marcus maps every evaluation criterion and submission requirement to a response section. This becomes your proposal blueprint — everything you need to address, in the order the agency wants to see it.

Draft each section with Marcus

Work through the proposal section by section. Ask Marcus to draft each one based on the solicitation requirements and your company profile. Review and revise each draft — adding your company-specific details, real project references, and voice — before moving to the next.

Run a final compliance check

Before submitting, ask Marcus: "Does my proposal address every requirement in the compliance matrix?" He'll identify gaps, missing certifications, or non-responsive sections you need to fix. Then submit through the agency's designated system.

// FAQ

Common Questions

Does GovScout Pro write my proposal for me?
▾
Marcus generates AI-assisted drafts and outlines — a starting point, not a finished product. He builds the structure, drafts sections based on your profile and the solicitation, and flags compliance requirements. You review, revise with your company's real details, and submit. The final proposal is always yours.
What documents should I upload?
▾
Upload the full solicitation (RFP, RFQ, or IFB), the Statement of Work or PWS, Section L (Instructions to Offerors), Section M (Evaluation Criteria), any amendments, and Q&A documents. Your capability statement and past performance references are also helpful. The more complete your upload, the more specific Marcus's outputs.
Are my documents secure?
▾
Yes. When you upload a document, your browser sends it directly to your chosen AI provider (OpenAI, Anthropic, Groq, or Gemini). GovScout Pro's servers never receive or store your documents. Your sensitive proposal materials stay between you and your AI provider.
Can GovScout Pro guarantee my bid will win?
▾
No. GovScout Pro helps you research, qualify, and prepare federal contract bids. It does not guarantee contract awards and does not replace legal, compliance, or contracting officer guidance. AI-generated content should always be reviewed before submitting to any government agency.
Do I need my own AI key?
▾
Yes. GovScout Pro uses your own AI provider key (OpenAI, Anthropic/Claude, Groq, or Gemini). This keeps your documents in your control — they go directly from your browser to your AI provider, not through our servers. New provider accounts typically include free credits to get started.
