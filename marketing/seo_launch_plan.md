# SEO Launch Plan

**Objective:** Capture high-intent small-business federal contracting research demand while using accurate, non-guaranteed messaging.

## SEO Position

GovScout.pro should own the practical middle ground between raw SAM.gov browsing and expensive consulting: software-assisted research and first-draft preparation for small businesses. Pages should answer real process questions before offering the product as a helpful workflow.

## Content Cluster

### Pillar: Set-Aside Research Basics

Supporting articles:

- SDVOSB opportunity research: where to start
- WOSB opportunity research: a practical workflow
- HUBZone opportunity research: what to verify
- 8(a) opportunity research: questions for your advisor and contracting office

All certification content should direct readers to authoritative SBA or agency sources for eligibility and program rules.

## On-Page Requirements

- Use one descriptive H1 and plain-language headings aligned to user questions.
- Include a visible statement that SAM.gov is the official opportunity source.
- Link every education page to one appropriate product CTA and related resources.
- Add FAQ schema only for questions visibly answered on the page and reviewed for accuracy.
- Add `SoftwareApplication` schema only after pricing, availability, and feature claims match production.
- Avoid structured-data ratings, reviews, awards, or organization credentials without documented proof.

## Measurement Plan

| Metric | Why It Matters |
| --- | --- |
| Organic impressions and non-branded clicks by theme | Shows whether intent pages are discoverable |
| Landing-page CTA click rate | Tests message-to-action alignment |
| Account-start conversion rate by landing page | Identifies commercially useful intent |
| Qualified activation event after signup | Separates traffic from actual product value |
| AppSumo referred conversion and refund reasons | Improves deal-page promises and onboarding |
| Support questions mentioning expectations | Surfaces copy that is unclear or overstated |

## SEO Launch Checklist

- Confirm canonical domain, indexability, XML sitemap, robots rules, and analytics ownership.
- Replace or reconcile existing page copy that makes unverified guarantees.
- Publish accurate title, description, H1, FAQ, and footer disclaimer.
- Validate all certification and data-source claims before indexing targeted pages.
- Submit sitemap and monitor Search Console after production launch approval.
