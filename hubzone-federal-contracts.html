// THE HUBZONE ADVANTAGE

Two Ways HUBZone
Opens Federal Doors

HUBZone certification isn't just a set-aside program — it gives you a competitive pricing advantage on full-and-open contracts that no other small business certification provides.

📊 The 10% price evaluation preference

On full-and-open competition contracts, the government evaluates HUBZone bids as if they're 10% lower than submitted. If you bid $110,000 and a non-HUBZone competitor bids $100,000, you're evaluated at $99,000 — making you the apparent low bidder. This applies even when you're not in a set-aside.

HUBZone set-aside contracts

Contracting officers can set contracts aside exclusively for HUBZone firms when there are at least two qualified HUBZone businesses that can meet the requirement. These are restricted competition — only HUBZone-certified firms can bid. With less competition than open market contracts, your win probability is structurally higher.

HUBZone eligibility requirements

  • Principal office must be located in a Historically Underutilized Business Zone
  • At least 35% of employees must reside in a HUBZone
  • Small business under SBA size standards for your NAICS code
  • At least 51% owned and controlled by U.S. citizens, a Community Development Corporation, an agricultural cooperative, an Alaska Native Corporation, or an Indian tribe
  • Certified through SBA at certify.sba.gov (annual recertification required)

// FAQ

Common Questions

How do I check if my location qualifies as a HUBZone?
▾
Use SBA's HUBZone map at maps.certify.sba.gov/hubzone/map. Enter your principal office address to see if it falls within a designated HUBZone. HUBZone maps are updated periodically — zones can be added or removed.
What is the 35% employee residency requirement?
▾
At least 35% of your total employees must live in a HUBZone — not necessarily the same HUBZone where your principal office is located. Any qualifying HUBZone counts. This is a continuing requirement — you must maintain it to keep your certification active, and SBA can audit compliance.
Can HUBZone firms also have other certifications?
▾
Yes, and it's advantageous. HUBZone + SDVOSB, HUBZone + 8(a), HUBZone + WOSB — stacking certifications expands the pool of set-aside contracts you can compete on. GovScout Pro lets you enter all your certifications and scores every opportunity against your full eligibility profile.
Does the 10% price preference apply to all federal contracts?
▾
The 10% price evaluation preference applies to full-and-open competition contracts. It does not apply to set-aside contracts (where only HUBZone firms can bid) or contracts awarded under other set-aside programs. It's most valuable in large open-competition procurements where price is a significant evaluation factor.
