Yes. If a service-disabled woman veteran owns 51% of the business, she can qualify for both WOSB/EDWOSB and SDVOSB certifications simultaneously. These stack — you can compete for both WOSB set-asides and SDVOSB set-asides, dramatically increasing your opportunity pool.
The SBA eliminated the third-party certification option in 2020. All WOSBs and EDWOSBs must now be certified directly through the SBA's Certify.sba.gov portal. Previously accepted third-party certifications from organizations like NWBOC are no longer valid for federal WOSB set-asides.
WOSB certification is ongoing but requires an annual certification update through the SBA's portal confirming you still meet eligibility requirements. You must also recertify when ownership or management changes significantly.
The SBA publishes a list of NAICS codes where women-owned firms are underrepresented or substantially underrepresented in federal contracting. EDWOSB set-asides are available for "substantially underrepresented" codes, while WOSB set-asides cover the broader "underrepresented" list. The SBA updates this list periodically — check the current list at sba.gov before bidding.
