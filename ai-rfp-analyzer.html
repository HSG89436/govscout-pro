// THE PROBLEM

Federal RFPs Are Written
To Confuse You.

The average federal solicitation runs 50–200 pages. It's full of FAR and DFARS clause references, performance work statements written in bureaucratic language, and buried requirements that can disqualify your bid if you miss them. Most small businesses don't have the staff — or the experience — to decode this efficiently.

📋
Buried compliance requirements
FAR clauses, insurance minimums, bonding requirements, and mandatory forms are scattered throughout. Missing any one of them can get your bid disqualified before it's even read.
🎯
No way to quickly assess fit
Without reading the entire document, you can't tell if the opportunity is actually appropriate for your business — or if the incumbent has it locked up before you start.
🚩
Hidden red flags
Unrealistic timelines, scope creep language, unusual insurance requirements, or past performance thresholds that eliminate most competitors — these only show up if you read carefully.

No. When you upload a document, your browser sends it directly to your chosen AI provider (OpenAI, Anthropic, Groq, or Gemini). GovScout Pro's servers never receive, see, or store your documents. This is by design — your sensitive bid documents stay between you and your AI provider.

▾
